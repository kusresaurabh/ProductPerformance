# Overview

The **Product Performance Dashboard** provides a detailed analytical view of how different products perform across various business metrics. This dashboard helps stakeholders evaluate product success, monitor sales performance, and identify opportunities to optimize product strategy.

By combining multiple performance indicators into a single interactive dashboard, business users can quickly understand which products are driving revenue and which ones require improvement.

---

# Business Objective

Organizations manage large product portfolios, making it challenging to identify top-performing products and underperforming ones. Without proper analysis, it becomes difficult to optimize inventory, pricing strategies, and marketing efforts.

The **Product Performance Dashboard** was designed to address these challenges by providing clear visual insights into product-level metrics.

The dashboard helps decision-makers answer important business questions such as:

• Which products generate the highest revenue?
• Which product categories perform best?
• How do different products compare in terms of sales performance?
• Which products may require strategic improvements?

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The dashboard highlights several key performance metrics used to evaluate product success.

Important KPIs displayed in the dashboard include:

• Total Product Sales
• Revenue generated by each product
• Product-level performance metrics
• Category-wise product performance
• Overall contribution of products to business revenue

These KPIs allow stakeholders to quickly assess product performance at both detailed and aggregated levels.

# Dashboard Components

The **Product Performance Dashboard** contains multiple visualization components that help users analyze product-related metrics efficiently.

### Product Sales Overview

This section provides a high-level view of product sales performance, helping users understand how different products contribute to overall revenue.

---

### Category-wise Performance Analysis

This visualization compares performance across different product categories. It allows stakeholders to identify:

• Top-performing categories
• Categories generating the highest revenue
• Categories with growth opportunities

---

### Product Comparison

Bar or column charts allow users to compare products based on key metrics such as:

• Sales volume
• Revenue contribution
• Product popularity

These visual comparisons help identify leading products and those requiring improvement.

---

### Distribution Analysis

Pie or donut charts illustrate how total revenue is distributed across different products or categories.

This helps business teams understand:

• Revenue share of each product
• Market dominance of certain product categories

---

### Interactive Filters

The dashboard includes interactive filters (slicers) that allow users to explore data dynamically.

Users can filter data based on:

• Product categories
• Product names
• Business segments
• Other available attributes

These filters enable deeper exploration of product performance.

# Data Preparation

The dataset used for this dashboard was prepared using **Power Query** in Power BI.

Data preparation steps included:

• Importing the dataset into Power BI Desktop
• Cleaning and validating data fields
• Handling missing values
• Standardizing data formats
• Preparing product-level attributes for analysis

These steps ensured the data was accurate and suitable for analytical reporting.

---

# Data Modeling

Power BI’s data modeling capabilities were used to structure relationships between tables and optimize performance.

The modeling process included:

• Establishing relationships between product and sales tables
• Creating calculated columns where necessary
• Implementing DAX measures for product metrics
• Optimizing the model for efficient querying

---

# DAX Measures

DAX calculations were implemented to compute important product performance metrics.

Examples include:

Total Sales = SUM(Sales[Amount])

Product Count = COUNT(Product[ProductID])

Average Product Revenue = AVERAGE(Sales[Amount])

These measures update dynamically based on filters applied in the dashboard.
